# D&D 5th Edition -- Combat Rules Reference

> Quick-reference for combat mechanics during session play. Source: Player's Handbook Chapter 9, Dungeon Master's Guide Chapter 8.

---

## The Combat Cycle

Combat proceeds in rounds. Each round, every combatant takes one turn in initiative order. One round represents approximately 6 seconds of in-game time.

### Order of Operations

1. **Determine Surprise** -- DM decides which side is surprised based on Stealth vs. Passive Perception.
2. **Establish Positions** -- Where is everyone when combat begins?
3. **Roll Initiative** -- All combatants roll 1d20 + Dexterity modifier. Ties broken by Dexterity score, then simultaneous resolution.
4. **Take Turns** -- Each combatant acts in initiative order (highest to lowest).
5. **Begin Next Round** -- After all turns, start a new round. Repeat until combat ends.

---

## Action Economy

On your turn, you can perform:

| Action Type | Usage | Examples |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Action** | 1 per turn | Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Ready, Search, Use an Object |
| **Bonus Action** | 1 per turn (if you have one) | Off-hand attack, certain spells, class features, Cunning Action |
| **Reaction** | 1 per round | Opportunity attack, Shield spell, Readied action, Uncanny Dodge |
| **Movement** | Up to your speed | Walk, climb, swim, fly (if capable). Can be split up during your turn |
| **Free Interaction** | 1 per turn | Draw a weapon, open a door, pick up a dropped item |
| **Object Interaction** | Uses Action or free (DM discretion) | More complex manipulations |

### Important Rules

- You cannot take two Actions unless a feature specifically grants one (Action Surge, Haste).
- You cannot take two Bonus Actions in a single turn, even if you have multiple features that use them.
- Unspent movement does not carry over between turns.
- You can move through ally space but not enemy space (unless two sizes different).

## Attack Rolls

### Melee Attack

1. Roll 1d20 + proficiency bonus + Strength modifier (or Dexterity for finesse weapons).
2. Compare to target's Armor Class (AC).
3. If the total equals or exceeds AC, the attack hits.
4. Roll damage as indicated by the weapon ( Strength modifier for melee).

### Ranged Attack

1. Roll 1d20 + proficiency bonus + Dexterity modifier.
2. Disadvantage if within 5 feet of a hostile creature (unless you have Crossbow Expert).
3. Long range imposes disadvantage beyond the weapon's first range increment.

### Critical Hits and Misses

- **Natural 20**: Always hits. Double all damage dice (roll twice, do not double modifiers).
- **Natural 1**: Always misses. Not an automatic fumble unless using house rules.
- **House rule (this campaign)**: Critical hits use max weapon dice plus a normal roll, with doubled modifiers.

| Armor Type | AC Calculation | Notes |
|------------|---------------|-------|
| None | 10 + Dexterity modifier | -- |
| Light | Base AC + Dexterity modifier | Padded 11, Leather 11, Studded Leather 12 |

## Conditions

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Blinded** | Auto-fail sight checks. Attacks have disadvantage. Attacks against you have advantage. |
| **Charmed** | Cannot attack the charmer. Charmer has advantage on social checks against you. |
| **Deafened** | Auto-fail hearing checks. |
| **Exhaustion** | Levels 1-6 with cumulative penalties: disadvantage on ability checks, speed halved, disadvantage on attacks/saves, HP halved, speed reduced to 0, death. |
| **Frightened** | Disadvantage on checks/attacks while source is in line of sight. Cannot willingly move closer. |
| **Grappled** | Speed becomes 0. Ends if grappler is incapacitated or effect removes creature beyond reach. |
| **Incapacitated** | Cannot take actions or reactions. |
| **Invisible** | Impossible to see without special sense. Attacks against you have disadvantage. Your attacks have advantage. |
| **Paralyzed** | Incapacitated, auto-fail Str/Dex saves. Attacks against you have advantage. Melee hits are criticals. |
| **Petrified** | Transformed to inanimate substance. Weight x10. Resistant to all damage. |
| **Poisoned** | Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. |
| **Prone** | Disadvantage on attacks. Melee attacks against you have advantage, ranged attacks have disadvantage. |
| **Restrained** | Speed 0. Attacks have disadvantage. Attacks against you have advantage. Disadvantage on Dex saves. |
| **Stunned** | Incapacitated, auto-fail Str/Dex saves. Attacks against you have advantage. |
| **Unconscious** | Incapacitated, auto-fail Str/Dex saves. Attacks have advantage. Melee hits are criticals. Drop held items, fall prone. |

## Flanking (Optional -- DMG p. 251)

When a creature and at least one ally are on opposite sides of a target, both gain advantage on melee attacks against that target.

**House rule (this campaign)**: Flanking is active. Position matters.

---

## Concentration

- Casting a concentration spell ends any previous concentration spell.
- When you take damage while concentrating, make a Constitution saving throw (DC 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher).
- You automatically succeed on DC 10 or lower.
- Multiple sources of damage: separate saves for each source.
- Concentration ends if: you cast another concentration spell, you drop to 0 HP, you die, you are incapacitated, the DM decides the spell ends due to environmental factors.

---

## Death and Dying

1. When HP drops to 0, you fall unconscious and begin making death saving throws.
2. On your turn, roll 1d20 (no modifiers): 10+ is a success, 9 or less is a failure.
3. Three successes: you stabilize. Three failures: you die.
4. Natural 1: Counts as two failures. Natural 20: Regain 1 HP and become conscious.
5. Taking damage while at 0 HP: Any damage counts as one death save failure. A critical hit counts as two failures.
6. Healing restores consciousness if it brings you above 0 HP.

---

## Healing

- Hit Dice: Spend during short rest to recover HP. Roll the die + Constitution modifier.
- Long Rest: Recover all HP. Recover half your total Hit Dice (minimum 1).
- Potion of Healing: 2d4+2 HP. (Greater: 4d4+4, Superior: 8d4+8, Supreme: 10d4+20)
- **House rule (this campaign)**: Drinking a potion is a bonus action. Administering to another creature is an action.
