# D&D 5th Edition -- Spellcasting Rules Reference

> Quick-reference for spell mechanics during session play. Source: Player's Handbook Chapter 10, Dungeon Master's Guide Chapter 8.

---

## Spellcasting Basics

### Spell Slots

Spells are cast using spell slots. A spell's level ranges from 0 (cantrips) to 9. You can cast a spell using a slot of equal or higher level, which may produce enhanced effects (cast at higher level).

| Spell Level | Slot Level | Recovery |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Cantrip | None (always available) | Never expended |
| 1st | 1st-level slot | Long rest (or Arcane Recovery, etc.) |
| 2nd | 2nd-level slot | Long rest |
| 3rd | 3rd-level slot | Long rest |
| 4th-9th | Corresponding slot | Long rest |

### Casting a Spell

1. **Check components** -- Verbal (V), Somatic (S), Material (M). If you cannot fulfill all components, you cannot cast the spell.
2. **Spend the slot** -- Unless a cantrip or a feature allows free casting.
3. **Resolve the spell** -- Roll to hit (for attack spells) or the target rolls a save.

## Spell Components

| Component | Requirement | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| **Verbal (V)** | Speak the incantation | Cannot cast while gagged or in an area of magical silence. |
| **Somatic (S)** | Gesture with at least one free hand | Cannot cast while bound or both hands occupied (unless War Caster feat). |
| **Material (M)** | Specific material or a spellcasting focus | If no cost is listed and no cost is specified, a focus replaces it. If a cost is listed (e.g., "a diamond worth 300 gp"), the component must be provided. Components are not consumed unless the spell says so. |

## Spell Attack Rolls vs. Saving Throws

### Spell Attack Roll

Caster rolls: 1d20 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier.

- If the total meets or exceeds the target's AC, the spell hits.
- Critical hits apply (natural 20).

### Saving Throw

Target rolls: 1d20 + appropriate ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient).

- The spell specifies which ability save (Dex, Con, Wis, etc.).
- The DC is: 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier.
- If the save meets or exceeds the DC, the effect is reduced or negated as described by the spell.

## Concentration

A spell with the concentration tag requires the caster's focus to maintain:

- **One at a time**: You can only concentrate on one spell at a time. Casting another concentration spell ends the first.
- **Damage check**: When you take damage while concentrating, make a DC 10 or half-damage Constitution save (whichever is higher).
- **Environmental factors**: Rough conditions (storm at sea, being thrown from a horse) may require DC 10-20 Constitution saves at DM discretion.
- **Duration**: Concentration lasts up to the spell's maximum duration or until you lose concentration.

### Losing Concentration

- Casting another concentration spell
- Dropping to 0 HP or dying
- Being incapacitated
- Failing a Constitution save triggered by damage
- DM-determined environmental disruption

## Areas of Effect

| Shape | Measurement | Rules |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Cone** | Length from point of origin | Extends in the direction you choose. 53-square area for a 30-ft cone on a grid. |
| **Cube** | Side length from point of origin | Origin can be any point in the cube. |
| **Cylinder** | Radius and height from point of origin | Extends vertically. Affects creatures within the radius at any height up to the cylinder's height. |
| **Line** | Length and width from point of origin | Extends in a straight line. 5 ft wide unless specified. |
| **Sphere** | Radius from point of origin | Extends in all directions, including around corners. |

### Point of Origin

The caster chooses the point of origin for the area. The spell's shape extends from there. Some areas require the point of origin to be the caster (self).

## Spell Ranges

| Range | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Self** | The caster is the target or the point of origin. |
| **Touch** | The caster must touch the target (melee spell attack or willing creature). |
| **X feet** | The maximum distance from the caster to the target or point of origin. |
| **Sight** | The target must be visible to the caster. |
| **Unlimited** | No range limit, but the target must be on the same plane (usually). |

## Ritual Casting

Some spells have the ritual tag. They can be cast as rituals:

- Casting time increases by 10 minutes.
- Does not expend a spell slot.
- The caster must have the ritual casting feature (Wizard, Cleric, Druid, etc.).
- Wizards can cast any ritual spell in their spellbook, even if not prepared.
- Other classes must have the spell prepared.

## Common Spell Interactions

### Counterspell

- 3rd-level reaction spell. Range: 60 feet.
- Interrupts a creature in the process of casting a spell.
- If the target spell is 3rd level or lower, it fails.
- If 4th level or higher, make an ability check using your spellcasting ability (DC 10 + spell's level). On success, the spell fails.

### Dispel Magic

- 3rd-level action spell. Range: 120 feet.
- Ends all spells of 3rd level or lower on a target.
- For each spell of 4th level or higher, make an ability check (DC 10 + spell's level). On success, that spell ends.
- Can be cast at higher levels to automatically dispel higher-level spells.

### Spell Effects and Stacking

- Multiple instances of the same spell on a target use the most potent instance.
- Different spells with similar effects stack (e.g., Bless and Guidance both add to rolls).
- Temporary HP from different sources does not stack; the creature keeps the higher value.

## Spellcasting in Combat

| Action | Cost | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Cast a cantrip | Action | Always available. |
| Cast a spell (1st+) | Action | Uses a spell slot. |
| Cast a bonus action spell | Bonus Action | You can only cast a cantrip with your action in the same turn. |
| Cast a reaction spell | Reaction | Triggered by a specific event (e.g., Shield when attacked). |
| Use a magic item | Action (usually) | Does not count as casting a spell. |

### The Bonus Action Spell Rule

If you cast a spell with a casting time of 1 bonus action, the only other spell you can cast that turn is a cantrip with a casting time of 1 action. This applies regardless of which comes first.

## Learning and Preparing Spells

### Wizards

- Learn 2 spells per wizard level for free. Can copy from scrolls/spellbooks (costs 50 gp + 2 hours per spell level).
- Prepare: Intelligence modifier + wizard level spells after a long rest.

### Clerics, Druids, Paladins

- Automatically know all spells on their class list.
- Prepare: Wisdom modifier + class level spells after a long rest.

### Bards, Rangers, Sorcerers, Warlocks

- Known spells list. Do not prepare; they always have their known spells available.
- Can swap one spell on level-up (sorcerer, ranger) or at certain intervals (bard, warlock).
