# NPC Templates -- Quick-Reference for Generating NPCs

> Use these templates to create memorable NPCs on the fly. Every NPC needs three things: a name, a motivation, and a secret.

---

## NPC Creation Checklist

For every NPC, determine:

1. **Name** -- Roll on the appropriate name table or invent one.
2. **Race and apparent age** -- What the party sees at a glance.
3. **Role** -- What function does this NPC serve? (Merchant, quest-giver, obstacle, ally, informer, villain)
4. **Personality trait** -- One dominant characteristic that makes them memorable.
5. **Motivation** -- What do they want right now? What do they want long-term?
6. **Secret** -- What are they hiding? Every NPC has a secret, even if it is minor.
7. **Voice** -- A speech pattern, verbal tic, or mannerism. Players remember how NPCs talk.
8. **Connection** -- How does this NPC connect to the party, the setting, or the plot?

## Innkeeper Template

**Function**: Information hub, rest point, social encounters.

| Attribute | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Name style | First name + inn name ("Greta of the Silver Eel") |
| Personality | Boisterous, gossip-hungry, dour and suspicious, motherly, miserly |
| Motivation | Keep the inn profitable, protect regulars, stay out of trouble (or cause it) |
| Secret | Hides fugitives in the cellar, waters down the ale, owes money to a local crime boss, is a retired adventurer |
| Voice | Loud and jolly, quiet and watchful, speaks in proverbs, has a catchphrase |
| Knowledge | Local rumors (70% accurate), traveler news (50% accurate), underground contacts (if friendly) |

### Sample Innkeepers

- **Marta Thickhand** -- Half-orc proprietress of the Rusty Anchor. Speaks in a gravelly boom, knows every smuggler on the coast. Secret: She is hiding her son from the city guard (he stole from a noble).
- **Fennwick Bottles** -- Gnome owner of the Half-Pint Haven. Nervous, constantly polishing glasses. Knows more than he lets on. Secret: He is a former Red Sash informant who fled his handlers.
- **Helga Ironkeg** -- Dwarf matron of the Anvil's Rest. Arms like tree trunks, no patience for troublemakers. Secret: The inn's cellar leads to an ancient dwarven crypt she has been quietly excavating.

## Merchant Template

**Function**: Supplies, price negotiation, potential quest hooks through rare items.

| Attribute | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Name style | Full name + trade ("Piotr Vasek, Purveyor of Fine Silks") |
| Personality | Smooth-talking, shrewd, desperate to sell, honest to a fault, exotic and mysterious |
| Motivation | Make profit, acquire rare goods, find a buyer for something dangerous |
| Secret | Selling stolen goods, smuggling contraband, is secretly a spy, has a cursed item in stock |
| Voice | Fast-talking, uses foreign words, haggling is a ritual, overly formal |
| Inventory | Common goods (reliable), rare goods (markup 200-500%), one unique item (plot hook) |

### Sample Merchants

- **Solkir the Blue** -- Tiefling trader in exotic reagents. Speaks softly, never blinks. Secret: He imports reagents from a lich's garden and does not ask questions about the side effects.
- **Dame Agatha Wheelwright** -- Human antique dealer. Elderly, refined, surprisingly sharp. Secret: She fences stolen artwork for the Thieves' Guild and uses the proceeds to fund an orphanage.
- **Grakk** -- Half-orc blacksmith. Gruff but fair. Makes weapons that are functional, never pretty. Secret: He is the last surviving member of a disgraced noble house and forges his family crest into every blade.

## Quest-Giver Template

**Function**: Hook the party into adventure, provide rewards, advance the plot.

| Attribute | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Name style | Title + name ("Lord Ashworth", "Sister Meriel") |
| Personality | Desperate, authoritative, evasive, earnest, testing the party |
| Motivation | Solve a problem they cannot handle alone, further a hidden agenda, test potential allies |
| Secret | The quest is a setup, the reward is cursed, they caused the problem, they are not who they claim to be |
| Voice | Formal and commanding, hesitant and uncertain, cold and calculating, warm but guarded |
| Reward | Gold (standard), magic item (1 rarity above party level), favor (social currency), information (plot advancement) |

### Sample Quest-Givers

- **Commander Lyra Blackthorn** -- Captain of the Narwell Watch. Sharp-featured, perpetually exhausted. Offers 200 gp per missing person recovered. Secret: She knows more about the disappearances than she is sharing because her superior is compromised.
- **Father Mordecai** -- Old priest of Pelor in Millbridge. Trembling hands, watery eyes. Begs the party to find the children taken from the village. Secret: The children were not taken -- they ran away to escape his cult, which he leads in secret.
- **The Hooded Stranger** -- Met on the road at midnight. Gender unclear beneath the cloak. Offers a map to a legendary treasure. Secret: The map is genuine, but the treasure is a prison for something ancient and hungry. The stranger needs the party to open the seal.

## Guard/Soldier Template

**Function**: Obstacle, authority figure, combat encounter, source of procedural information.

| Attribute | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Name style | Rank + surname ("Sergeant Miller", "Corporal Vane") |
| Personality | By-the-book, easily bribed, bored and lazy, aggressive and suspicious |
| Motivation | Do the job, get paid, go home alive |
| Secret | On the take, part of a conspiracy, hates their commander, is actually a deserter from another army |
| Voice | Crisp and military, bored drawl, casual and friendly, hostile and demanding |
| Challenge | 1-2 guards: low threat. Squad (4-8): moderate. Garrison: overwhelming, avoid combat. |

### Sample Guards

- **Corporal Brannick** -- Human guard at the Narwell gate. Red-nosed, smells of cheap wine. Will accept a "donation" to forget irregularities. Secret: He reports everything to the Scarlet Brotherhood in exchange for medicine for his sick daughter.
- **Sergeant Thessa Ironheart** -- Dwarf veteran of the Greyhawk Wars. Lost an eye at the Battle of Critwall. By-the-book and incorruptible. Secret: She is searching for the officer who betrayed her unit and has a list of suspects.
- **Private Fen** -- Young human recruit. Nervous, eager, clearly in over his head. Secret: He joined the guard to find his missing brother, who is one of the disappeared.

## Sage/Scholar Template

**Function**: Lore dumps, research assistance, magical knowledge, plot exposition.

| Attribute | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Name style | Honorific + surname ("Professor Calloway", "Archivist Morvin") |
| Personality | Absent-minded, pedantic, paranoid about their research, genuinely helpful |
| Motivation | Complete their research, protect their findings, gain funding or rare materials |
| Secret | Their research is dangerous, they stole it from a colleague, they are being blackmailed, they have discovered something they wish they had not |
| Voice | Academic jargon, tangential lectures, precise and clinical, excited rambling about their specialty |
| Knowledge | Their specialty (95% accurate), related fields (70%), outside specialty (30% or roll on a table) |

### Sample Sages

- **Archivist Fendril** -- Elf librarian at the Free City's University. Has not left the stacks in 40 years. Knows everything about pre-Greyhawk Wars treaties. Secret: He is slowly going mad from reading a cursed grimoire that whispers to him at night.
- **Dr. Miriam Ashworth** -- Human physician and alchemist. Practical, no-nonsense. Studies the effects of marsh gases on the human body. Secret: She has been dosing her patients with experimental remedies without their knowledge.
- **Lorekeeper Thistle** -- Gnome historian specializing in the Mistmarsh. Cheerful, speaks very fast, always carries chalk. Secret: She has mapped an entrance to the ancient ruins beneath the marsh but has not told anyone because she is afraid of what she found there.

## Villain Archetypes

### The Hidden Mastermind

- Appears as an ally, employer, or neutral party.
- Motivation: Long-term power, revenge, or ideological goals.
- Secret: They are behind the party's current problems.
- Voice: Calm, reasonable, even warm -- until the reveal.
- Reveal timing: After at least 3 sessions of interaction.

### The Obvious Threat

- The party knows they are the villain from the start.
- Motivation: Territory, resources, or sheer malice.
- Secret: They have a sympathetic motivation the party can discover.
- Voice: Booming, threatening, memorable catchphrases.
- Use: To unify the party and provide clear goals.

### The Tragic Antagonist

- Someone the party could have been friends with under different circumstances.
- Motivation: Loss, desperation, or being cornered with no good options.
- Secret: They want to be stopped but cannot bring themselves to surrender.
- Voice: Tired, bitter, occasionally wistful for what could have been.
- Purpose: Moral complexity, player investment, memorable confrontation.

## Voice and Mannerism Quick Tables

### Speech Patterns (d10)

1. Uses third person ("The barkeep thinks you should leave.")
2. Constantly asks rhetorical questions
3. Speaks in incomplete sentences. Trails off...
4. Over-enunciates every word precisely
5. Uses nautical terms regardless of context
6. Speaks exclusively in whispers
7. Repeats the last few words of each sentence, each sentence.
8. Addresses everyone by a title they make up on the spot
9. Cannot finish a sentence without being interrupted by their own thought
10. Speaks in rhyme when stressed or lying

### Physical Mannerisms (d10)

1. Constantly adjusting clothing or armor
2. Never makes eye contact
3. Always has something in their hands (eating, fidgeting, cleaning)
4. Stands too close when talking
5. Perpetually scanning the room while speaking
6. Hands never stop moving -- gesturing, counting, fidgeting
7. Rocks back and forth on their heels
8. Tilts their head at an angle when listening
9. Covers their mouth when speaking
10. Finger-guns at everyone, always
