# Target Species

Species targeted by the F/V Morning Star in the Gulf of Alaska and Bering Sea fisheries. Each species entry includes identification, market grades, handling requirements, and regulatory notes. All catch must be sorted by species and recorded in the eVTR system before the end of each day.

## Pacific Cod (Gadus macrocephalus)

### Identification
- Brown to gray body with irregular dark markings on the back and sides
- Three separate dorsal fins, two anal fins
- Distinct barbel (whisker) on the chin
- Lateral line is light, nearly white
- Typical weight: 5-25 lbs, up to 40 lbs for large males

### Market Grades
- **J-cut**: Headed and gutted with collar removed. Standard grade for shore-based processing
- **Eagle cut**: Headed and gutted with collar on. Higher yield, preferred for fillet production
- **Whole**: Less common but some markets pay premium for large whole fish
- **Price range**: $0.35-0.55/lb ex-vessel (headed and gutted), fluctuates seasonally

### Handling Requirements
- Bleed immediately by cutting gill arches. Bleed tank for 10 minutes
- Head and gut within 20 minutes of landing. Keep the kidney line clean
- Ice at 1:1 ratio in fish hold. Target core temp below 35F
- Do not stack more than 18 inches deep in ice without dividers

### Regulatory Notes
- Managed under the Gulf of Alaska Groundfish Fishery Management Plan
- 2026 Western GOA TAC: 27,431 mt (J gear sector allocation: 18.3%)
- Halibut PSC limit applies to trawl-caught cod
- Season dates vary by sector and gear type. Verify current openings with NOAA Fisheries Alaska Region

## Sablefish (Anoplopoma fimbria)

### Identification
- Dark gray to black skin, white flesh
- Two dorsal fins, the first short and spiny, the second long and soft
- Scales are very small, skin appears almost scaleless
- Long, slender body. Typical weight: 4-12 lbs, up to 25 lbs
- Often called "black cod" in the market, though it is not a cod

### Market Grades
- **Headed and gutted (H&G)**: Standard form. Price premium for larger fish
- **Whole round**: Some Asian markets pay premium for whole fish
- **Size grades**: Under 3 lbs, 3-5 lbs, 5-7 lbs, 7+ lbs. Price increases with size
- **Price range**: $3.50-5.50/lb ex-vessel (H&G), highly variable by season and size

### Handling Requirements
- Bleed immediately. Sablefish quality degrades faster than cod
- Handle carefully -- the skin is delicate and tears reduce grade
- Ice at 1:1 ratio. Keep separate from other species in the hold
- Freeze at sea if equipped: blast freeze to -20F core temperature for long-term storage

### Regulatory Notes
- Managed under the IFQ system. Vessel must hold valid IFQ permit for the regulatory area
- 2026 GOA IFQ season: March 14 through November 15
- Area 3A (Central GOA) and Area 3B (Western GOA) have separate quota allocations
- Longline gear required in most sablefish IFQ fisheries. Check regulations for trawl sablefish retention
- Verify current IFQ pounds available through NOAA Fisheries RAM division

## Walleye Pollock (Gadus chalcogrammus)

### Identification
- Olive green to brownish back, silvery sides and belly
- Three dorsal fins, two anal fins (similar to cod but no chin barbel)
- Prominent white lateral line
- Large eyes relative to body size
- Typical weight: 1-4 lbs

### Market Grades
- **Whole round**: Standard for surimi and fish stick production
- **Headed and gutted**: Premium for fillet production
- **Roe**: Pollock roe (eggs) are a high-value product in Japanese markets. Roe season typically January-March
- **Price range**: $0.08-0.15/lb ex-vessel (whole round). Roe premium: $0.50-1.50/lb additional

### Handling Requirements
- Pollock are soft-fleshed and damage easily. Handle with care
- Process quickly -- quality degrades fast in warm conditions
- RSW (refrigerated seawater) systems work well for pollock
- Target temperature below 34F. Do not exceed 38F

### Regulatory Notes
- Largest US fishery by volume. Managed under GOA and BSAI Groundfish FMPs
- AFA (American Fisheries Act) vessels have specific allocations
- Halibut and salmon PSC limits apply to pollock trawl fisheries
- Pollock season is split into A season (January-April) and B season (August-November)
- In-season management may close areas. Monitor NOAA Fisheries announcements daily

## Pacific Halibut (Hippoglossus stenolepis)

### Identification
- Diamond-shaped flatfish, both eyes on the right side of the head
- Dark brown to olive green on the eyed side, white on the blind side
- Tail is slightly forked (distinguishes from arrowtooth flounder, which has a square tail)
- Very large -- typical commercial size: 20-100 lbs, can exceed 300 lbs
- Flesh is firm, white, and flaky

### Market Grades
- **Headed and gutted, dressed (H&G)**: Standard market form with tail on
- **Fletches**: Large boneless fillets, each side of the fish yields two fletches
- **Size grades**: Under 10 lbs, 10-20 lbs, 20-40 lbs, 40-60 lbs, 60+ lbs
- **Price range**: $4.00-7.00/lb ex-vessel (H&G), depending on size and season

### Handling Requirements
- Bleed immediately by cutting a V-shaped incision in the gill arch. This is critical for white flesh quality
- Do not gut halibut by making a belly slit -- instead, remove gills and pull viscera through the gill cavity to preserve belly integrity
- Ice at 1:1 ratio. Do not allow fish to rest directly on the deck or on other fish without ice
- Halibut are graded partly on appearance. Bruised or cut fish receive lower prices

### Regulatory Notes
- Managed by the International Pacific Halibut Commission (IPHC) and NOAA Fisheries
- IFQ system: vessel must hold valid halibut IFQ for Area 3A or applicable area
- 2026 IFQ season: March 14 through November 15 (verify with IPHC annual regulations)
- Size limit: 32 inches minimum. Undersized halibut must be returned to the water immediately with minimal handling
- Halibut caught as bycatch in groundfish fisheries counts against PSC allowances, not IFQ. May not be retained without IFQ

## Yellowfin Sole (Limanda aspera)

### Identification
- Right-eyed flatfish with a rounded body shape
- Dark olive to brownish on the eyed side with yellowish tints
- Rough scales along the lateral line (ctenoid scales)
- Typical weight: 0.5-2 lbs
- Pectoral fin on the eyed side has a dark spot at the base

### Market Grades
- **Whole round**: Primary market form for processing
- **Headed and gutted**: Some premium for select markets
- **Price range**: $0.10-0.20/lb ex-vessel (whole round)

### Handling Requirements
- Flatfish are delicate. Stack carefully to prevent crushing
- Ice adequately. RSW systems work well
- Sort from arrowtooth flounder at landing -- arrowtooth has soft, unmarketable flesh when cooked

### Regulatory Notes
- Managed under BSAI Groundfish FMP. Primarily a Bering Sea fishery
- Harvested as part of the flatfish complex allocation
- Low PSC rate, but halibut bycatch limits still apply
