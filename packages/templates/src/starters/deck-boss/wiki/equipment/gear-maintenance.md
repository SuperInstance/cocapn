# Gear and Equipment Maintenance

Deferred maintenance sinks boats. Every piece of equipment on the F/V Morning Star has a maintenance schedule, and every schedule exists because something failed at the worst possible time in the past. Follow the schedules. Log the work. If you see something wrong, report it immediately.

## Main Engine -- Cummins KTA38-M0

The main engine is the heart of the vessel. If it fails at sea, you are drift fishing whether you want to be or not.

### Daily Checks
- Oil pressure: 55-65 PSI at cruise RPM (1500 RPM). Log any deviation
- Coolant temperature: 175-195F at cruise. Alarm at 210F. Shutdown at 215F
- Oil level on dipstick: check cold, before startup. Add only approved SAE 15W-40
- Belts: visual check for cracking, glazing, or proper tension. Deflection should be 1/2 inch at midpoint
- Look for leaks: oil, coolant, fuel, and exhaust. Any leak gets reported and logged

### Fuel Consumption Baseline
- Idle (400 RPM): 3.2 GPH
- Cruise (1500 RPM): 28-32 GPH depending on load and sea state
- Full load (1800 RPM): 42-48 GPH
- Track fuel burn per trip. A sudden increase in fuel consumption often indicates hull fouling, propeller damage, or engine issues

## Hydraulic System

The hydraulic system runs the deck winches, net reel, crane, and steering gear. A hydraulic failure during hauling is an emergency.

### System Specifications
- Operating pressure: 2,000-2,500 PSI
- Relief valve set: 2,800 PSI
- Reservoir capacity: 120 gallons
- Fluid type: ISO VG 46 hydraulic oil

### Weekly Checks
- Fluid level in reservoir sight glass. Top off if below the full mark
- Filter indicator on return line filter. Replace when indicator shows restricted
- Inspect all hoses for blistering, cracking, or abrasion. Pay attention to fittings and connections
- Check for leaks at all cylinder seals, valve bodies, and hose connections
- Test backup manual override valve for steering

### Service Intervals

| Interval | Task | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| 500 hours | Hydraulic filter replacement | Use OEM filters only. Record pressure drop before replacement |
| 1,000 hours | Hydraulic oil analysis | Send sample to lab. Check for water content, particulate count, and additive depletion |
| 2,000 hours | Full hydraulic oil change | Flush system before refilling. Replace all filters at the same time |
| As needed | Hose replacement | Any hose showing exterior damage is replaced immediately. No exceptions |

### Winch-Specific Maintenance
- Main winch wire rope: inspect daily for broken strands. Replace when broken strands exceed 6 in any one lay or when diameter reduction exceeds 10%
- Wire rope lubrication: apply wire rope lubricant every 30 days during active fishing
- Net reel bearings: grease weekly with marine-grade lithium grease
- Crane wire: inspect before each use. Replace every 3 years regardless of condition
- All shackles, swivels, and links: magnetic particle inspection annually

## Refrigeration System (RSW)

The RSW system maintains fish quality. If the chill system is down, the fish hold becomes a warm room and the catch loses value by the hour.

### System Specifications
- Capacity: 60 tons refrigeration
- Compressor: Carrier 06E, 40 HP
- RSW temperature target: 30-32F
- Hold temperature target: below 35F at fish core

### Daily Checks During Fishing
- Compressor oil level and pressure
- Discharge pressure: 180-220 PSI (varies with seawater temperature)
- Suction pressure: 20-35 PSI
- Seawater flow through condenser: verify adequate flow, check strainer
- RSW temperature: verify hold temperature is dropping or stable at target
- Listen for unusual sounds: knocking, squealing, or rattling indicate problems

### Service Intervals

| Interval | Task | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| 250 hours | Compressor oil change | Use approved refrigeration oil. Check for acid content |
| 500 hours | Condenser cleaning | Backflush with freshwater. Inspect tubes for fouling |
| 1,000 hours | Refrigerant charge check | Verify superheat and subcooling values. Add refrigerant if low |
| Annual | Expansion valve adjustment | Verify proper superheat settings. Adjust if hunting or starving |
| Annual | Leak test | Electronic leak detector on all fittings, valves, and connections |

## Deck Gear

### Trawl Nets
- Inspect after every tow. Document any torn mesh, broken riblines, or chafed areas
- Small repairs done on watch. Large repairs done in port. Net never goes in the water with known damage
- Codend mesh size: measured weekly with the legal mesh gauge. Must meet minimum legal size for the fishery
- Net design modifications logged in the gear record book with date and rationale

### Longline Gear
- Inspect gangions after every haul. Replace bent hooks, stripped hooks, and chafed gangions immediately
- Mainline: check for chafe at the connection points between skates. Replace worn sections
- Snap-on gear: verify snap strength. Replace any snap that shows deformation
- Bait hooks stored in the bait freezer. Thaw only what is needed for the next set
- Skates are numbered. Log catch by skate number for every set

### Pot Gear (if applicable)
- Inspect frames for cracks, bent bars, and damaged escape panels
- Replace tunnel netting when openings exceed legal size or show significant wear
- Check buoy lines for chafe and UV degradation. Replace annually
- Pot lids must function properly. A pot that does not fish correctly is wasted gear
