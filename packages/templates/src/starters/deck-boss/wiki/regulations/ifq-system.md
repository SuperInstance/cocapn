# Individual Fishing Quota (IFQ) System

The IFQ system is the foundation of catch share management in Alaska fisheries. Understanding it is not optional -- it is how you stay legal, stay profitable, and stay fishing. This guide covers the IFQ programs relevant to the F/V Morning Star's operations in the Gulf of Alaska.

## Overview

The Individual Fishing Quota system allocates specific poundage of harvest rights to individual permit holders. Each permit holder receives a share of the Total Allowable Catch (TAC) for a species in a specific regulatory area. The share is expressed as a percentage of the TAC, and the actual pounds vary year to year based on TAC adjustments.

IFQ species managed under this system in Alaska include:
- Pacific halibut (Areas 2C, 3A, 3B, 4A, 4B, 4C, 4D, 4E)
- Sablefish (Southeast, West Yakutat, Central GOA, Western GOA, Bering Sea, Aleutian Islands)

IFQ is not a right to fish. It is a right to harvest a specific amount of fish, subject to season dates, area regulations, gear restrictions, and all other applicable rules.

## How IFQ Works

### Quota Allocation

1. NOAA Fisheries sets the TAC annually for each species and area, based on stock assessments and recommendations from the North Pacific Fishery Management Council (NPFMC)
2. Each IFQ permit holder owns a percentage of the TAC for their area/species combination
3. The actual pounds available are calculated: Permit Share % x TAC = IFQ Pounds
4. Pounds are allocated as Adjusted Quota (AQ) after any deductions for Community Development Quota (CDQ) programs

### Quota Categories

| Category | Description | Holder Type |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| QS (Quota Share) | The underlying percentage of TAC. Transferable, but with restrictions | Individual or corporation |
| IFQ | The annual poundage derived from QS for a given year | Issued annually to QS holders |
| ACE (Annual Catch Entitlement) | Annual harvest rights under the halibut/sablefish IFQ program | Can be leased independently of QS |

### Transfer and Leasing

- QS transfers require NOAA Fisheries approval. Transfer restrictions apply based on area, vessel size, and ownership caps
- QS cannot be transferred to non-US citizens or foreign corporations
- IFQ leasing rules vary: halibut IFQ may be leased only by initial issuers in certain areas. Sablefish IFQ leasing rules differ by area
- All transfers recorded through NOAA Fisheries Restricted Access Management (RAM) division
- Processing plant QS ownership is capped at specific percentages by area

## Compliance Requirements

### What You Must Do

1. **Carry your IFQ permit** -- The original permit must be aboard the vessel while fishing for IFQ species. Copies are not acceptable
2. **Land only against your quota** -- You cannot land more pounds than your IFQ allocation for the species and area. Overages result in deduction from the following year's allocation at a 2:1 penalty ratio
3. **Report all catch** -- eVTR (electronic Vessel Trip Report) submissions are mandatory. Deadlines: daily during the trip, final report within 7 days of landing
4. **Allow observer access** -- If your vessel is selected for observer coverage, you must carry the observer. Interference with the observer is a federal offense
5. **Fish in the correct area** -- IFQ is area-specific. Catching halibut in Area 3B against your Area 3A quota is illegal. Know your boundaries
6. **Use legal gear** -- IFQ species have gear restrictions. Halibut: longline only (with limited exceptions). Sablefish: longline or pot gear depending on area
7. **Maintain records** -- All catch records, landing receipts (fish tickets), and transfer documentation must be retained for 3 years minimum

### eVTR Reporting

Electronic Vessel Trip Reports are required for all IFQ trips.

| Data Point | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| Trip start | Date, time, port of departure, permit number |
| Daily catch | Species, pounds (round weight), area fished, statistical area |
| Trip end | Date, time, port of landing, total catch by species |
| Submission | Daily during trip via approved eVTR system |
| Final report | Within 7 calendar days of landing |
| Accuracy | Must match fish ticket data. Discrepancies flagged for review |

### Landing Requirements

- All IFQ fish must be landed at a registered processor
- Fish tickets (landing receipts) are generated at the processor and submitted to NOAA Fisheries and ADFG
- Weights on fish tickets are rounded whole pounds. No decimal weights
- IFQ holders must sign the fish ticket at landing
- Multiple landings per trip are allowed but each landing generates a separate fish ticket
- Retained IFQ fish may not be transferred at sea. All transfers occur at registered processing facilities

## Area Boundaries (Gulf of Alaska)

### Area 3A -- Central Gulf of Alaska
- Northern boundary: 60 degrees 10 minutes N latitude (Cape Suckling to Gore Point)
- Western boundary: 147 degrees W longitude
- Southern boundary: US EEZ boundary
- Major ports: Kodiak, Homer, Seward, Cordova
- This is the F/V Morning Star's primary halibut IFQ area

### Area 3B -- Western Gulf of Alaska
- Eastern boundary: 147 degrees W longitude
- Western boundary: 159 degrees W longitude (roughly, with specific coordinates at the Alaska Peninsula)
- Major ports: Kodiak, Sand Point, King Cove
- Sablefish IFQ for the Morning Star covers both 3A and 3B

### Statistical Areas

IFQ reporting uses NMFS statistical areas within each IFQ area. Key areas for the Morning Star:
- Area 610: Shumagin Islands (western GOA)
- Area 620: Chirikof Island (central-western GOA)
- Area 630: Kodiak (central GOA)
- Area 640: Yakutat (eastern GOA)
- Area 650: Southeastern (eastern GOA)

## Penalties

IFQ violations are federal offenses prosecuted by NOAA Office of Law Enforcement (OLE).

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Fishing without IFQ permit | Seizure of catch and gear, permit revocation, criminal prosecution |
| IFQ overage | 2:1 deduction from following year's allocation. Repeat offenses: permit sanctions |
| False reporting | Criminal prosecution, permit revocation, fines up to $100,000 |
| Fishing in wrong area | Catch seized, permit sanctions, fines |
| Observer interference | Federal criminal offense, fines, imprisonment |
| Unreported discards | Catch violation, permit sanctions, potential criminal charges |
