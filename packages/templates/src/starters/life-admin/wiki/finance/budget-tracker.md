# Budget Tracker Guide

A practical approach to personal finance that Life Admin uses to help you stay
on top of bills, track spending, and build savings without spreadsheet anxiety.

## Philosophy

Budgeting should reduce stress, not create it. The goal is awareness, not
restriction. You should know where your money goes, plan for what matters, and
never be surprised by a bill. Life Admin handles the tracking and reminding;
you make the decisions.

## Monthly Budget Structure

### Fixed Expenses (predictable, same every month)

These are your non-negotiables. Track them, automate them, and review
quarterly for optimization opportunities.

- Rent / mortgage payment
- Car payment or transit pass
- Insurance (auto, renters/home, health)
- Internet and phone
- Subscriptions and memberships (gym, streaming, software)
- Loan payments (student, personal)
- Childcare or tuition

### Variable Expenses (predictable range, fluctuates)

These have natural variation. Set a monthly target and track actual spend.

- Groceries
- Utilities (electric, gas, water)
- Transportation (gas, parking, ride-share)
- Dining out and coffee
- Household supplies and personal care
- Clothing
- Entertainment and hobbies
- Gifts and donations

### Savings and Goals

Pay yourself first. Automate transfers on pay day.

- Emergency fund contribution
- Retirement / investment contribution
- Short-term savings (vacation, home repair, holiday fund)
- Specific goal savings tracked separately

### Buffer

Keep a 5-10% buffer for unexpected expenses. If unused, roll it into savings
at the end of the month.

## Bill Management System

### Bill Calendar

Every bill gets an entry with:

- Name and amount (or estimated range)
- Due date (day of month)
- Payment method (auto-pay, manual, check)
- Account it draws from
- Annual review date (to renegotiate or cancel)

### Reminder Schedule

Life Admin uses a tiered reminder system:

- **7 days before**: First reminder with amount and account info
- **3 days before**: Follow-up if not yet marked as paid
- **1 day before**: Urgent reminder for manual-pay items only
- **Day of**: Final alert for anything still outstanding

### Subscription Audit

Quarterly review of all subscriptions:

1. List every active subscription with monthly cost
2. Mark each as: essential, valuable, nice-to-have, forgotten
3. Cancel anything in "forgotten" category immediately
4. Evaluate "nice-to-have" — keep only if used in the last 30 days
5. Calculate total monthly savings from cancellations
6. Redirect savings to a goal

## Budget Categories and Targets

Example monthly allocation (adjust to your situation):

| Category         | Target    | Method                |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------------------|
| Housing          | 30%       | Fixed, auto-pay       |
| Transportation   | 10%       | Fixed + variable      |
| Food             | 15%       | Variable, weekly track|
| Utilities        | 5%        | Variable, seasonal    |
| Insurance        | 8%        | Fixed, auto-pay       |
| Savings          | 15%       | Automated transfer    |
| Personal         | 7%        | Variable, discretionary|
| Entertainment    | 5%        | Variable, discretionary|
| Buffer           | 5%        | Overflow to savings   |

## Annual Financial Review

Once a year, go deeper:

- Net worth calculation (assets minus liabilities)
- Insurance coverage review
- Subscription and recurring expense audit
- Tax withholding check
- Emergency fund health check (3-6 months of expenses)
- Retirement contribution adequacy
- Big expenses coming in the next 12 months

## How Life Admin Helps

- Tracks bill due dates and sends tiered reminders
- Maintains a running monthly budget vs. actual comparison
- Flags unusual spending patterns
- Projects savings goal completion dates
- Runs the quarterly subscription audit
- Prepares the annual financial review summary
- Never shares financial data outside your private repo

## Warning Signs to Watch

- Using credit cards to cover monthly expenses (not rewards optimization)
- Savings rate dropping below 10% for more than two months
- Buffer consumed more than twice in a quarter
- Any bill going to the 1-day reminder stage regularly
- Subscription creep — total monthly subscriptions growing quarter over quarter
