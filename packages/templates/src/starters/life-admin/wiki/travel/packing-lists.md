# Travel Packing Lists

Comprehensive packing lists and travel preparation guide. Life Admin generates
customized packing lists based on your destination, season, trip duration, and
who is traveling. These are the master templates.

## Pre-Trip Checklist

Before you even start packing, run through this list 2-4 weeks before departure:

### Documents and Bookings

- [ ] Confirm flight reservations and check-in times
- [ ] Confirm hotel or accommodation bookings
- [ ] Reserve rental car or arrange ground transportation
- [ ] Check passport expiration (must be valid 6+ months beyond return date)
- [ ] Verify visa requirements for destination
- [ ] Print or save digital copies of: passport, ID, insurance cards, itinerary,
      hotel confirmations, rental car confirmation, emergency contacts
- [ ] Notify bank of travel dates (prevent card fraud blocks)
- [ ] Check travel insurance coverage
- [ ] Share itinerary with emergency contact

### Home Preparation

- [ ] Arrange pet care (boarding, house sitter, or drop-in visits)
- [ ] Arrange plant watering schedule
- [ ] Set thermostat appropriately (or program schedule)
- [ ] Hold mail delivery or arrange pickup
- [ ] Set out trash and recycling for pickup day
- [ ] Clean out refrigerator of perishables
- [ ] Take out all garbage
- [ ] Lock all windows and doors, set alarm
- [ ] Leave a key with a trusted neighbor or friend
- [ ] Set automatic light timers if desired

### Health Preparation

- [ ] Refill prescriptions to cover trip duration plus buffer
- [ ] Pack medications in carry-on (never checked bags)
- [ ] Check vaccination requirements for destination
- [ ] Pack first aid basics: bandages, pain reliever, antihistamine, motion
      sickness medication, any personal essentials
- [ ] Know location of nearest hospital or clinic at destination

## Master Packing List

### Essentials (Always Pack)

- Passport and/or government ID
- Wallet: credit cards, cash (local currency if international), insurance cards
- Phone and charger
- Portable battery pack
- Headphones or earbuds
- Keys (home, car)
- Sunglasses
- Prescription medications (3-day buffer beyond trip length)
- Glasses or contact lenses + supplies
- Tissues and lip balm

### Clothing — Base (3-5 Day Trip)

Adjust quantities proportionally for longer trips. Plan to re-wear items and
do laundry for trips over 5 days.

- Underwear: number of days + 1
- Socks: number of days + 1
- T-shirts / tops: one per day, mix of casual and nice
- Pants / shorts: 2-3 pairs
- Sleepwear: 1-2 sets
- Light jacket or sweater: 1 (even in warm climates, for air conditioning)
- Comfortable walking shoes: 1 pair (wear on plane)
- Dressier shoes: 1 pair (if needed for dining or events)

### Seasonal Additions

#### Warm Weather

- Swimwear: 2 sets
- Sandals or flip-flops
- Sun hat or cap
- Lightweight, breathable fabrics
- Reef-safe sunscreen SPF 50+ (if beach destination)
- Insect repellent
- Light, long-sleeved shirt for sun protection

#### Cold Weather

- Warm coat or jacket (water-resistant preferred)
- Thermal base layer top and bottom
- Warm sweater or fleece: 2
- Warm socks (wool or synthetic, not cotton)
- Gloves or mittens
- Warm hat or beanie
- Scarf or neck gaiter
- Waterproof boots or shoes
- Hand and foot warmers (for extended outdoor time)

#### Rainy Season

- Rain jacket or poncho
- Compact travel umbrella
- Waterproof shoes or boots
- Quick-dry clothing
- Plastic bags for wet clothes and electronics
- Waterproof phone case or dry bag

### Toiletries

- Toothbrush and toothpaste
- Deodorant
- Shampoo and conditioner (travel size or solid bars)
- Body wash or soap
- Face wash and moisturizer
- Sunscreen
- Razor and shaving cream
- Hair styling products
- Any skincare routine items
- Feminine hygiene products
- Nail clippers and file

### Electronics

- Phone charger (wall adapter + cable)
- Laptop and charger (if working)
- Portable battery pack (10,000mAh minimum)
- Universal power adapter (if international)
- Camera and memory cards (if not using phone)
- E-reader or tablet + charger
- Download offline maps and entertainment before departure

### Travel with Kids

For each child, in addition to the standard list:

- Favorite comfort item (stuffed animal, blanket)
- Age-appropriate entertainment for travel (books, games, tablet)
- Snacks for travel day (more than you think you need)
- Change of clothes in carry-on (for spills and accidents)
- Child medications: fever reducer, allergy medicine, motion sickness
- Child ID and health insurance card
- Car seat or booster (or arrange rental at destination)
- Stroller or baby carrier
- Diapers, wipes, and changing supplies (if applicable)
- Child-safe sunscreen and insect repellent
- Sippy cup or water bottle
- Small first aid kit with bandages and antiseptic

## Packing Strategies

### The Roll Method

Roll clothes instead of folding. Saves space, reduces wrinkles, and makes it
easier to see what you packed.

### Outfit Planning

Plan outfits by day rather than packing individual items. This prevents
overpacking and ensures everything matches. Life Admin can help generate
outfit plans based on your planned activities.

### The One-Suitcase Rule

For trips under 7 days, aim for one carry-on per person. Benefits:
- No baggage fees
- No waiting at baggage claim
- No lost luggage risk
- Forces intentional packing

### Packing Cubes

Use packing cubes to organize by category:
- Cube 1: Tops
- Cube 2: Bottoms
- Cube 3: Underwear and socks
- Cube 4: Toiletries
- Cube 5: Electronics and chargers
- Cube 6: Documents and valuables
