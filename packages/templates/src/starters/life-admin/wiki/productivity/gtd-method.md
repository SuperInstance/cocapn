# Getting Things Done (GTD) Method

The GTD method by David Allen is the backbone of the Life Admin task system.
It is built around a simple principle: your mind is for having ideas, not
holding them. Every commitment, idea, and obligation should live outside your
head in a trusted system.

## The Five Steps

### 1. Capture

Collect everything that has your attention. Anything that might require action
goes into your inbox — a single collection point. Do not organize yet, just
capture.

- Work tasks, personal errands, ideas, commitments, reading list items
- If it might need action, it goes in the inbox
- The inbox is not a to-do list — it is a staging area
- Empty your head completely. If you think of it, capture it

### 2. Clarify

Process your inbox item by item. For each item, ask: What is this? Is it
actionable?

- **Not actionable**: File as reference, someday/maybe, or trash it
- **Actionable in under 2 minutes**: Do it now
- **Actionable, single step**: Assign to a project or context
- **Actionable, multi-step**: Define as a project, identify the next action

### 3. Organize

Sort clarified items into the right buckets:

- **Next Actions**: The very next physical step for each project. Context-tagged
  (@computer, @phone, @errands, @home)
- **Projects**: Any outcome requiring more than one step. Track these separately.
- **Waiting For**: Delegated items you are waiting on others for. Include date
  delegated and expected response.
- **Someday/Maybe**: Things you want to do but not now. Review weekly.
- **Calendar**: Time-specific commitments only. Not a to-do list.
- **Reference**: Non-actionable information you need to keep.

### 4. Reflect

Review your system regularly to maintain trust:

- **Daily**: Review calendar and next actions for the day
- **Weekly**: Full weekly review (this is critical and non-negotiable)
  - Process all inboxes to zero
  - Review all projects and next actions
  - Review waiting-for items — follow up if stale
  - Review someday/maybe list — promote or demote
  - Look ahead 2-4 weeks on the calendar
  - Get clear on priorities for the coming week

### 5. Engage

Do the work. With a trusted system in place, you can confidently choose what
to work on based on:

- **Context**: Where are you and what tools do you have?
- **Time available**: How much time do you have before the next commitment?
- **Energy level**: High energy for hard tasks, low energy for admin work
- **Priority**: What is most important right now?

## How Life Admin Applies GTD

Life Admin integrates GTD by:

- Maintaining a persistent inbox that you can add to anytime via conversation
- Automatically clarifying items through dialogue — asking what the next action is
- Organizing tasks by project, context, and priority
- Running weekly reviews with habit stats, budget summaries, and goal tracking
- Providing daily engagement suggestions based on your calendar, energy, and context

## Common GTD Mistakes

- Using the calendar as a to-do list (calendar is for time-specific events only)
- Skipping the weekly review (the system breaks down without reflection)
- Keeping things in your head instead of capturing them
- Defining projects without identifying the next action
- Letting the inbox pile up — process it at least every few days
- Overcomplicating contexts — keep them simple and practical

## The Two-Minute Rule

If an action takes less than two minutes, do it immediately. This prevents
small tasks from clogging your system and consuming more organizational
overhead than the task itself warrants. Life Admin will flag two-minute items
and suggest knocking them out during capture processing.
