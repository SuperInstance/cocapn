# Habit Tracker Guide

A practical guide to building and maintaining healthy habits. Life Admin tracks
your habits, celebrates your streaks, and gently course-corrects when things
slip — without the guilt trip.

## Why Habits Matter

Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. Small actions, repeated
consistently, produce remarkable results over time. You do not rise to the level
of your goals; you fall to the level of your systems. The habit tracker is your
system.

## Habit Categories

### Physical Health

- **Hydration**: 8 glasses of water per day. Track by glass or bottle.
- **Exercise**: 3-5 sessions per week. Mix of cardio, strength, and flexibility.
- **Sleep**: 7-9 hours per night. Track bedtime and wake time.
- **Movement breaks**: Stand and stretch every 60-90 minutes during work.
- **Steps**: 8,000-10,000 steps per day. Or equivalent activity.
- **Medication**: Daily supplements and prescriptions taken on schedule.

### Nutrition

- **Meals**: Regular meal times. No skipping breakfast.
- **Fruits and vegetables**: 5+ servings per day.
- **Meal prep**: Prepare meals in advance (target: 3+ days per week).
- **Mindful eating**: At least one meal per day without screens.
- **Water with meals**: Replace sugary drinks with water at meals.

### Mental Wellness

- **Meditation**: 5-20 minutes per day. Morning is ideal.
- **Journaling**: Write for 5 minutes. Gratitude, reflection, or brain dump.
- **Reading**: 20+ minutes per day. Books, not feeds.
- **Screen-free time**: 1 hour before bed. No phone, no tablet.
- **Outdoor time**: 20+ minutes outside daily, weather permitting.

### Productivity

- **Inbox zero**: Process email inbox to zero (or a defined max).
- **Deep work**: 1+ block of focused, uninterrupted work per day.
- **Planning**: Review tomorrow before ending today.
- **Weekly review**: Complete GTD weekly review every Sunday.

### Social and Relationships

- **Quality time**: Dedicated time with partner or family. Present, not distracted.
- **Reach out**: Contact one friend or family member per day.
- **Date night**: Weekly or biweekly dedicated time with partner.
- **Play**: Unstructured fun with kids (if applicable) — 30+ minutes.

## Building New Habits

### Habit Stacking

The most reliable way to build a new habit is to attach it to an existing one.
The formula: "After I [current habit], I will [new habit]."

Examples:
- After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for 5 minutes.
- After I brush my teeth at night, I will take my magnesium.
- After I sit down at my desk, I will write my top 3 priorities.
- After I finish dinner, I will prepare tomorrow's lunch.

### Start Small

The biggest mistake is going too big too fast. Start with a version so easy you
cannot say no:

- Want to exercise daily? Start with 5 minutes.
- Want to read more? Start with one page.
- Want to meditate? Start with 60 seconds.
- Want to journal? Start with one sentence.

Build the identity first ("I am someone who meditates"), then increase the
duration or intensity.

### Two-Day Rule

Never miss twice. Missing one day is an accident. Missing two days is the start
of a new (bad) habit. If you miss a day, make the next day your priority.

### Environment Design

Make good habits easy and bad habits hard:

- Put vitamins next to your toothbrush
- Set out exercise clothes the night before
- Keep a water bottle on your desk
- Put your phone in another room during deep work
- Remove junk food from the house (do not rely on willpower)

## Tracking With Life Admin

Life Admin tracks habits with a flexible system:

### Daily Check-ins

Brief check-in at configurable times (default: morning and evening). Life Admin
asks about yesterday's habits and today's plans. Takes under 60 seconds.

### Streak Tracking

Life Admin tracks consecutive days for each habit. Streaks are celebrated:
- 3 days: Nice start
- 7 days: One week strong
- 14 days: Building momentum
- 21 days: Habit forming
- 30 days: One month — this is who you are now
- 100 days: Part of your identity

### Adaptive Reminders

Life Admin adjusts reminder timing and frequency based on your patterns:
- If you usually do a habit in the morning, reminders come in the morning
- If a habit is slipping (missed 2+ days), check-ins increase gently
- If a habit is strong (30+ day streak), check-ins decrease to avoid fatigue
- Quiet hours respected — no habit reminders after 10pm or before 7am

### Weekly and Monthly Stats

- Weekly: completion percentage per habit, streak status, trend direction
- Monthly: habit consistency over time, best and worst performing habits,
  correlation between habits (do you exercise more on days you meditate?)

## Handling Slips

When you miss habits, Life Admin responds with empathy:

- No guilt, no shame, no disappointment
- Simple acknowledgment: "Yesterday was a tough one. That happens."
- Gentle redirect: "What would help today feel more manageable?"
- Pattern check: "I noticed exercise slipped the last three Wednesdays.
  Is Wednesday a hard day? Want to adjust the plan?"

## When to Add or Remove Habits

Add habits when:
- You have maintained your current habits at 80%+ for 4+ weeks
- You have identified a specific behavior that would improve your life
- You can attach it to an existing habit via habit stacking

Remove habits when:
- A habit has become automatic and no longer needs tracking
- A habit is causing anxiety rather than supporting wellbeing
- Your life circumstances have changed and the habit no longer fits
- You are consistently below 50% for more than 3 weeks — simplify, do not struggle
